Interest in IT

My interest in IT is more in the field video games and cyber security. The interest of video games began in early childhood, approximately 4 years old with Pokemon on the Gameboy Advance. Having zero knowledge about video games, aimlessly wandering around on a bike and demolishing opposing pokemon at the cost of my own, i fell in love with games. Though i like playing the video games themselves, the behind-the-scenes of the creation of video games was captivating however, the bts of the process were highlights, the time consumed in order to create the game, character models, create a storyline (if there is any), debug and many more operations in order for a game to be created were not completely shown, it was clipped to make the documentaries and process look interesting and fun. Later on in life, about 10 years down the track cyber security caught my interest, though having no background of IT through high school, security, is what I wish to pursue at RMIT. Most people state that they chose RMIT because it’s more convenient for them as commuting is easier. My justification for choosing RMIT is because their IT course is what i wanted, flexibility within the field of IT and provides me the opportunity to major in cyber security. During my studies at RMIT, I don’t expect to come out knowing everything about the workforce and having all the experience required for the job that i wish for. However, i do expect to have some knowledge and be able to apply that knowledge to the place i work and be able to complete the tasks that i have been allocated and attempt to go above and beyond what is required.

Ideal Job

The ideal job for myself would be in the field of information security. The position of security analyst at Peoplebank Australia monitor the network for security breaches and explore for a breach when a breach occurs. Security analysts must also protect sensitive information with the use of firewalls and data encryption programs. Security analyst at Peoplebank Australia is appealing to me because Peoplebank Australia is about the clients and as a security analyst, one has to provide security for the clients and utilises the skills learnt from university and more. The job advertisement is for Peoplebank Australia and the position requires people to have a good understanding of how internal and external networks communicate, have the ability to create and tweak rules and make sure that everything reaches specifications or requirements.
